Lychee

I once claimed that lychee, rambutan, and mamoncillo were basically the same thing. This page is a more careful answer.

Taxonomic context

These fruits all belong to the same plant family:

  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Sapindaceae (the soapberry family)

Sapindaceae contains around 140 genera and nearly 2,000 species, including maples, horse chestnuts, and several tropical fruits. The similarity between lychee, rambutan, and mamoncillo lives at the level of family, not species.

Selected genera and species

Sapindaceae (soapberry family)

  • Litchi chinensis — lychee (archetype: translucent flesh, floral sweetness) video
  • Dimocarpus longan — longan (very close to lychee; less aromatic) wiki
  • Nephelium lappaceum — rambutan (lychee-like; thick hairy rind) video
  • Nephelium mutabile — pulasan (lychee-like; denser flesh than rambutan) wiki
  • Melicoccus bijugatus — guinip / mamoncillo (partially lychee-like; thin pulp, large seed) video
  • Pometia pinnata — korlan / matoa (lychee-like; Southeast Asian) wiki
  • Blighia sapida — ackee (Sapindaceae; toxic unless prepared) wiki
  • Paullinia cupana — guaraná (economically important stimulant seed) wiki
  • Sapindus spp. — soapberries (saponin-rich; detergents, cosmetics) wiki
  • Acer spp. — maples (sap → maple syrup; wood) wiki
  • Aesculus spp. — buckeyes / horse chestnuts (wood; ornamental) wiki
  • Schleichera trijuga — kusum tree (source of Indian macassar oil) wiki
  • Koelreuteria, Cardiospermum, Ungnadia (ornamental genera) wiki

Related but outside Sapindaceae

  • Garcinia mangostana — mangosteen (Clusiaceae; similar flesh, thick rind) wiki
  • Annona spp. — custard apples (Annonaceae; segmented flesh, creamy texture) wiki

Pairwise taxonomic distances

Distances are measured as steps between taxa in the classification (Order → Family → Subfamily → Genus → Species). Lower values indicate closer evolutionary relationships.
