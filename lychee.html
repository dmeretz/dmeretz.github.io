Lychee

I once claimed that lychee, rambutan, and mamoncillo were basically the same thing. This page is a more careful answer.

Taxonomic context

These fruits all belong to the same plant family:

  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Sapindaceae (the soapberry family)

Sapindaceae contains around 140 genera and nearly 2,000 species, including maples, horse chestnuts, and several tropical fruits. The similarity between lychee, rambutan, and mamoncillo lives at the level of family, not species.

Selected genera and species

Litchi (lychee)

Litchi chinensis is the sole species in its genus. Native to southern China, it produces the familiar rough red shell and translucent white flesh.

Video reference: Fruits of Colombia – lychee

Nephelium (rambutan, pulasan)

The genus Nephelium includes rambutan (Nephelium lappaceum) and several closely related fruits. Rambutan differs from lychee mainly in the thick, hairy pericarp.

Video reference: Fruits of Colombia – rambutan

Melicoccus (mamoncillo / guinep)

Melicoccus bijugatus is native to the Caribbean and northern South America. The fruit has a thinner layer of edible pulp tightly bound to a large seed.

Video reference: Fruits of Colombia – mamoncillo

Dimocarpus (longan)

Longan (Dimocarpus longan) is often described as a “less perfumed” lychee. It is extremely close morphologically and chemically.

Conclusion

These fruits are not the same species, or even the same genus. But they are close relatives within Sapindaceae, shaped by similar evolutionary pressures. So the claim was wrong in detail, but not in spirit.

Pairwise taxonomic distances

Distances are measured as the number of steps between taxa in the classification (Order → Family → Subfamily → Genus → Species). Lower numbers indicate closer biological relationships.
